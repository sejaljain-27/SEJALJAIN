Dr. A.P.J. Abdul Kalam

Missile Man of India | Former President | Visionary Scientist

Early Life

super hero Avul Pakir Jainulabdeen Abdul Kalam was born on 15 October 1931 in Rameswaram, Tamil Nadu. He studied physics and aerospace engineering and went on to become one of India's most celebrated scientists.

Achievements

  • Chief architect of India's missile program (AGNI & PRITHVI)
  • 11th President of India (2002–2007)
  • Author of inspirational books like "Wings of Fire"
  • Awarded Bharat Ratna, India's highest civilian honor

Legacy

Dr. Kalam inspired millions of students with his humility, wisdom, and vision for a developed India. He passed away on 27 July 2015 while delivering a lecture—doing what he loved the most: teaching.

Quotes

"Dream is not that which you see while sleeping; it is something that does not let you sleep."
